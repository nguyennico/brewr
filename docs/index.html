The Supply Chain Capability Gap

Modern enterprises spend millions implementing global Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems and Advanced Planning Suites (APS). Yet, if you walk into almost any supply chain department, the actual day-to-day tactical decisions are still being run on fragile, slow, manual Excel workbooks.

Why? Because static enterprise software is built for standardization, not agility.

When your business encounters real-world disruptions—like changing shelf-life constraints, complex promotional buckets, or the need for fair-share demand allocations—legacy software forces your team to extract data into spreadsheets that break if a single column moves.

Why R? The Competitive Edge in Planning

We build custom planning solutions using R, an open-source, production-grade statistical programming language. By treating your supply chain logic as code rather than cell formulas, we unlock unprecedented operational advantages:

⚡ Speed & Scale at 10,000+ SKUs

Traditional spreadsheets grind to a halt when calculating forward stock projections and safety stock coverages across deep product portfolios. R processes millions of rows of supply chain data natively in seconds.

🔄 Absolute Reproducibility

No more hidden macro bugs or accidental cell deletions. An R-driven pipeline ensures that the exact same mathematical logic is applied to your data every single week, leaving a transparent, auditable trail from raw ERP data to final S&OP plan.

🌐 Native Integration & Open Source Freedom

R connects seamlessly with your existing data stack—whether fetching data from SQL warehouses, cloud APIs, or flat ERP dumps—without requiring expensive per-user software licenses or complex proprietary platforms.

Core Pillars of the planr Framework

Our consulting interventions leverage lightweight, functional programming architectures to target the three bottlenecks where traditional software fails:

  • Demand Bucket Automation: Programmatically parsing and aligning erratic demand signals, automatically splitting monthly macro targets into executable, weighted weekly buckets.
  • Replenishment & DRP Diagnostics: Algorithmic calculation of forward-looking projected inventory balances, dynamic transit pipeline tracking, and instant safety stock alert systems.
  • Constrained Allocation & Risk Management: Rules-based “fair-share” distribution engines to allocate inventory optimally during supply shocks, alongside automated tracking of short-shelf life (SSL) write-off exposures.
